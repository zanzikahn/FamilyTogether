## Pricing Guide

Use this guide to set fair point costs:

{% tabs %}
{% tab title="Small Rewards (10-20 pts)" %}
### Quick Wins

Rewards earnable in 1-2 days of tasks.

**Examples:**
- 15 min extra screen time (10)
- Small snack treat (10)
- Choose breakfast (15)
- 10 min later bedtime (15)
- Pick the music (10)
{% endtab %}

{% tab title="Medium Rewards (25-40 pts)" %}
### Weekly Goals

Rewards that take about a week of effort.

**Examples:**
- 30 min extra screen time (15)
- Choose dinner (25)
- Movie night pick (30)
- Special dessert (25)
- Skip one chore day (35)
- Small toy/item (40)
{% endtab %}

{% tab title="Large Rewards (50+ pts)" %}
### Big Goals

Rewards worth saving for over weeks.

**Examples:**
- Friend sleepover (60)
- Trip to favorite place (75)
- New video game (100)
- Bigger toy/item (80)
- Stay up 1 hour late (50)
- Day trip of choice (100)
{% endtab %}
{% endtabs %}

## Reward Strategy Tips

### Make It Motivating

{% hint style="success" %}
**Ask your kids what they want!**

They'll work harder for rewards they actually care about.
{% endhint %}

### Balance the Store

Good reward stores have:
- ‚úÖ Several small rewards (quick wins)
- ‚úÖ A few medium rewards (weekly goals)
- ‚úÖ 1-2 big rewards (long-term motivation)

### Keep It Fresh

- Add new rewards occasionally
- Remove rewards nobody wants
- Adjust prices based on popularity

### Be Realistic

Only offer rewards you're willing to give! Don't promise things you can't deliver.

---

## When Rewards Are Claimed

What happens when a child clicks on a reward:

1. Points are **instantly deducted**
2. A confirmation message appears
3. **You should honor the reward!**

{% hint style="danger" %}
**Important: Follow Through!**

If you don't give the reward after they "buy" it, kids will lose trust in the system. Always honor claimed rewards.
{% endhint %}
